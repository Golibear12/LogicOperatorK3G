Operational States: Major and Lower Modes
The State Lock-in Rule Major and Lower represent the operational state or the specific process algorithm of a solution. When you initiate a solution using the rules of a particular state (e.g., Major), you are strictly bound to its rules until the very end of that process. You cannot arbitrarily switch between Major and Lower rules mid-computation.
The Ultimate Goal The primary objective of both states is to achieve the lowest possible number of base elements while generating the highest possible order. Ideally, the process should reduce the entire sequence into a single, compact entity. Most sequences successfully collapse to an end result under either state.
State Switching (The Exception) Occasionally, relying solely on either Major or Lower mode might fail to push an equation to a proper, completely resolved final entity. In such specific scenarios, you are permitted to change the state to process the remaining unresolved portion of the equation. However, once the state is changed at that specific step, it becomes locked again. You cannot continuously toggle between states in the middle of a continuous operation.
